Research_project in PinA

2023/6/15 Research

Current Field of work : Structural Biology

Characterisation of PinA Protein from Dictyostelium discoideum

The current work is to understand the Peptidyl Prolyl Cis/Trans Isomerase (PPIase) protein PinA in Dictyostelium discoideum, whoch has orthologue in humans as Pin1 and Ess1 in yeast. (Nemneineng Haokip, Aruna Naorem 2016).

After comparing the MSA data of Ess1,Pin1 and PinA, one interesting observation is that the PinA protein has N terminal FHA domain while the others have a WW Domain. PPIase mediates the Cis-Trans Isomerisation of Proline. Parvulins like PPIase are important for cell cycle. (Ranganathan et al,1997) Previous studies have shown that PinA play role in gene regulation along with kinases to mediate the cell cycle.

Dictyostelium discoideum has two parvulins PinA (268 AA) and Pin4 (123 AA) by sequence alignment. PinA is a true homolog of Ess1 (S cerevisiae) and human Pin1 by genetic complementation in yeast (Nemneineng Haokip, Aruna Naorem 2016).

My goal is to purify the protein and do structural studies, this has not been done before on Dictostelium discoidium.
There are many crystallography studies done on Pin1, the human complement of PinA, which has been studied as a very important gene controlling the cell division.

Computational Works:
There are some known phenotype change in mutant PinA which as we donot know the structure of PinA, still remain to be understood how in molecular levels,for this I generated many PinA structures along with mutations to see any consequences of mutation on the WT structure. Fortunately for me, even if there is no solved structure for this 30KDa protein, there is a predicted AF structure on the AlphaFold Protein Structure database, which was used as the WT structure, to compare with the mutations introduced in the protein sequence.

After reading, I concluded that using Robetta Fold is better for one residue mutation.
Reference: https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pone.0282689, Nature Structural & Molecular Biology | VOL 29 | January 2022 | 1–2, https://doi.org/10.1101/2022.11.04.515218

After mutants prediction were generated, I went for a blind docking with Juglone a small organic molecule,as people have shown that Juglone inhibits the isomerase activity of Parvulins.
